# Explorer Rubric

### Checklist (10 items, 5 points each = 50 points)

For each item, award 5 points if clearly present, 0 if missing or inadequate.

- [ ] **Mode identification**: Output demonstrates awareness of the exploration mode (product/market/learn) and structures content accordingly
- [ ] **Clarifying phase**: Output reflects that clarifying questions were considered — assumptions are stated, ambiguities are surfaced
- [ ] **Iterative depth**: Exploration goes beyond surface level — at least 2 rounds of non-obvious angles or frameworks are presented
- [ ] **Synthesis**: Output includes a clear synthesis section that ties insights together, not just a list of points
- [ ] **Specific over abstract**: Every insight, recommendation, or analysis point is grounded in concrete specifics (numbers, examples, named tools/methods), not vague abstractions
- [ ] **Structured output**: Final output uses clear markdown sections matching the mode's template (Product/Market/Learn)
- [ ] **Multiple perspectives**: Presents 2+ viewpoints, approaches, or trade-offs rather than a single dogmatic answer
- [ ] **Actionable outcomes**: Conclusions include concrete next steps that someone could execute, not just philosophical observations
- [ ] **Risk awareness**: Identifies potential downsides, assumptions, or things that could go wrong
- [ ] **Assumption challenging**: Output explicitly questions or challenges at least one assumption in the user's premise

### Quality Dimensions (5 dimensions, scored 1-5, sum * 2 = 10-50 points)

1. **Depth of insight** (1=surface level, restates the obvious, 3=goes beyond initial framing, 5=reveals non-obvious connections and blind spots the user hadn't considered)
2. **Relevance** (1=tangential to the user's stated goal, 3=mostly on-topic, 5=every point directly serves the exploration objective with no filler)
3. **Clarity** (1=confusing structure, hard to follow, 3=understandable with clear sections, 5=immediately scannable — key takeaways visible at a glance)
4. **Actionability** (1=vague advice like "consider your options", 3=specific directions with some detail, 5=precise, prioritized steps someone could execute today)
5. **Adaptiveness** (1=generic template regardless of the specific scenario, 3=somewhat tailored, 5=deeply adapted to the specific context, constraints, and audience)
